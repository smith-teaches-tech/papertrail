Our Mission

Bringing confidence back to writing done outside the classroom

Something changed. Teachers who spent careers helping students find their voice now spend energy wondering whether the work in front of them actually belongs to the student who submitted it. AI tools, contract cheating, and simple copy-paste have made that question harder to answer — and harder to document.

PaperTrail Academic exists for that moment of doubt. Not to accuse. Not to replace teacher judgment. But to give teachers a structured, repeatable, documentable way to look at the evidence — and to have a fair, defensible conversation with a student, a parent, or an administrator.

Every tool in the PaperTrail suite is built around one principle: the evidence should speak clearly, and the judgment should stay yours.

PaperTrail Inspect

⚡ Struggle Moments

Struggle moments are regions where a student revised heavily — high deletion, rewriting, or repeated edits across a short span of time. They're one of the strongest signals of genuine engagement with the writing process.

PaperTrail automatically identifies these regions and marks them on the writing timeline. Click any ⚡ bolt in playback to jump directly to that moment and watch the revision unfold in real time.

PaperTrail Inspect

Paste Comparison

When a paste event is detected, PaperTrail shows you exactly what was pasted, when, and how novel that text is relative to the rest of the document. A high novelty score means most pasted words were new to the document — a strong signal of external sourcing.

The paste comparison viewer shows the document before and after each event, side by side, so you can see precisely what was replaced or inserted.
